Research Proposal – High-Frequency Sentiment from Order Flow

  • Core idea: trade-taker side as sentiment
    • Define sentiment from the aggressor side of the trade:
      • Buyer-initiated trades → positive sentiment (willingness to pay the spread to acquire the asset).
      • Seller-initiated trades → negative sentiment (willingness to pay the spread to offload the asset).
    • Economic intuition: aggressors reveal stronger beliefs or urgency than passive liquidity providers, so their direction is a natural proxy for short-horizon sentiment.
  • Volume-weighted sentiment to capture economic importance
    • Construct a volume-weighted order-flow measure, where each trade’s sign (buy/sell) is multiplied by its traded volume.
    • Economic intuition: larger aggressive trades are more likely to come from informed or high-conviction traders, so they should move prices more than small, possibly liquidity-motivated trades.
  • Normalization by recent traded volume (relative pressure)
    • Normalize volume-weighted order flow by recent total traded volume (e.g., past X minutes) to obtain relative buy/sell pressure.
    • Economic intuition: the same absolute buy volume is more informative in a thin, illiquid period than in a very active one; normalization controls for baseline liquidity and time-of-day effects.
  • Interpreting high relative buy/sell volume as pressure
    • High relative buy volume (large buyer-initiated volume relative to recent activity) = strong buying pressure, suggesting upward pressure on prices.
    • High relative sell volume = strong selling pressure, suggesting downward pressure.
    • Economic intuition: persistent net demand on one side of the book signals imbalanced beliefs and tends to push the price toward that side.
  • Partition by recent returns and use momentum as conviction
    • Split observations by recent return regime (e.g., positive vs. negative short-term returns) and interact with relative buy/sell pressure.
    • When buy pressure aligns with recent positive returns, interpret as trend-following, high-conviction continuation of existing information.
    • When buy pressure follows recent negative returns, interpret as contrarian/value trades, signaling conviction that prices have overreacted.
    • Economic intuition: the combination of order-flow direction, size, and recent price path allows us to distinguish mere noise from high-conviction information trading, and to test whether such trades predict future returns and volatility.
